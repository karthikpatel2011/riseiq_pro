# Product

## Register

product

## Users

College students (undergraduate, early postgrad) working on academic and personal projects — building their first serious portfolio, entering hackathons, preparing for placements or internships. Secondary: job seekers and startup founders using the same project-OS model. Primary device: laptop/desktop, desk or library setting. They arrive goal-oriented, often anxious about "doing it right," and need the tool to make them feel capable and structured — not overwhelmed or behind.

## Product Purpose

RiseIQ is an AI Project Operating System for students and colleges. Its flagship feature is an AI Copilot that guides a project from raw idea or problem statement through a 14-step pipeline — planning, documentation, presentation, defense — and auto-builds the student's portfolio and resume from real, completed work. Success = a student who arrived with a vague idea leaves with a documented, presentable, defensible project and a portfolio that proves it.

MVP surfaces: Student Dashboard · AI Copilot · Projects (workspaces) · Team Workspace · Portfolio.

## Brand Personality

Intelligent · Clean · Academic.

Trusted and institution-grade — the way GitHub feels authoritative to developers. Structured and purposeful, every screen designed to move real work forward. Not a social product, not a gamified learning app: a serious tool that respects the user's time and amplifies their capability.

## Anti-references

- **Generic EdTech** (Coursera, Udemy, standard LMS): boxy card grids, blue-heavy corporate palette, passive consumption UX, forgettable visual identity.
- **Social feed apps** (LinkedIn student mode): feed-centric layout, notification-driven engagement, performative progress bars, cluttered sidebars.
- **Flashy SaaS dashboards**: hero-metric layouts (big number + gradient card), glassmorphism, gradient text, decorative animations. Looks impressive on a Dribbble shot; feels hollow in daily use.
- **Government/university student portals**: table dumps, form-heavy, zero visual hierarchy, UX designed for administrators not students.

The specific failure to avoid: a clean surface that is still *generic* — indistinguishable from 100 other student tools. Cleanliness must be in service of a distinct, confident voice.

## Design Principles

1. **Work surfaces, not social stages.** Every screen accelerates a task. No engagement metrics, no feed mechanics, no performative widgets. If it doesn't help the student finish or improve their project, it doesn't belong on the screen.
2. **Intelligence made visible.** AI assistance is the core value prop — it should feel woven into the workflow, not bolted on as a sidebar chatbot. Copilot suggestions, pipeline stage guidance, and auto-generated content should appear where work happens.
3. **Earned confidence.** Students arrive uncertain. The UI should progressively reveal complexity: new users can start in one click; power users unlock depth through use. Never front-load the tool's full capability in one overwhelming screen.
4. **Institution-grade outputs.** The portfolio, resume, and project documentation RiseIQ produces will be shown to professors, recruiters, and judges. Every output surface must look polished enough to be taken seriously in those contexts.
5. **Decisive restraint.** "Clean" is only valuable when it's a deliberate choice, not a default. Pick one strong visual decision per screen and commit to it — in color, in type scale, in spatial emphasis. Restraint that makes no statement is just absence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Desktop-first responsive design (primary: laptop/1280px+; secondary: tablet 768px+; tertiary: mobile). Reduced-motion alternative required for all transitions. Sufficient contrast on all text including muted/secondary states.
